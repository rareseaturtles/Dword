Understanding Step 11

Step 11 involves seeking through prayer and meditation to improve our conscious contact with God as we understand Him, praying only for knowledge of His will for us and the power to carry that out. This step emphasizes the importance of spiritual growth and guidance in the recovery process.

Key Concepts

  • Spiritual Practice: Engaging in regular prayer and meditation to deepen spiritual awareness.
  • Conscious Contact: Developing a mindful and intentional relationship with a higher power.
  • Seeking Guidance: Asking for insight into the higher power's will and the strength to follow it.
  • Personalized Understanding: Recognizing that each individual's concept of a higher power is unique and personal.

How to Work Step 11

  1. Establish a Routine: Set aside dedicated time daily for prayer and meditation.
  2. Define Your Practice: Determine what forms of prayer and meditation resonate with you, acknowledging that spirituality is personal and can evolve over time.
  3. Seek Understanding: Focus your practice on gaining insight into your higher power's will for you.
  4. Request Strength: Pray for the power to carry out the guidance you receive.
  5. Maintain Openness: Stay receptive to spiritual experiences and insights that may arise.
  6. Reflect and Adjust: Regularly assess your spiritual practices and make adjustments as needed to deepen your connection.

Inspirational Quotes

"Sought through prayer and meditation to improve our conscious contact with God as we understood Him, praying only for knowledge of His will for us and the power to carry that out." — Alcoholics Anonymous

"Prayer and meditation are our principal means of conscious contact with God." — Twelve Steps and Twelve Traditions

Reflection

Consider how enhancing your spiritual connection can provide guidance and strength in your recovery journey. Reflect on the personal nature of your relationship with your higher power and how it influences your daily life.
